Solid Oxide Fuel Cell (SOFC) Development

Bosch Corporate Research | Thermal Engineering | CFD | System Simulation

Contributed to the development of high-temperature Solid Oxide Fuel Cell (SOFC) systems by leading thermal management, CFD simulations, system-level thermal modelling and simulation automation. The work focused on improving thermal efficiency, component integration and reducing engineering development time through advanced simulation methodologies.

Key Contributions

  • Thermal Management & Interface Optimization – Performed CFD simulations to optimize thermal and flow interactions between burners, reformers, heat exchangers, blowers and pumps operating at stack temperatures above 500°C.
  • Flow Distribution Optimization – Designed and optimized manifolds to achieve uniform anode and cathode gas distribution, improving burner performance and overall system reliability.
  • Simulation Automation Framework – Developed Python-based automation tools for geometry generation, meshing, solver setup, post-processing and report generation, significantly reducing simulation turnaround time.
  • System-Level Thermal Design – Developed 1D analytical models to predict heat losses, insulation performance and temperature distribution across the complete SOFC system under multiple operating conditions.
  • Thermal Design Optimization – Delivered engineering recommendations that improved component integration, thermal efficiency and supported rapid design iterations during product development.
